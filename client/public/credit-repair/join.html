Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to the most common questions about our credit repair service.

How long does credit repair take?

Most clients begin seeing initial results within 30-45 days as credit bureaus respond to our first round of disputes. The complete process typically takes 3-4 months, though complex cases may require additional time. Credit bureaus are legally required to investigate disputes within 30 days (in most cases), which creates our timeline for results.

Is credit repair legal?

Yes, credit repair is completely legal. Under the Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA), you have the legal right to dispute inaccurate information on your credit reports. Our service helps you exercise this right effectively through professional advocacy. We strictly follow all applicable laws including the Credit Repair Organizations Act (CROA) and the FCRA.

What types of items can you remove from my credit report?

We can help remove inaccurate, unverifiable, incomplete, or outdated negative items from your credit report. This includes late payments, collections, charge-offs, inquiries, repossessions, judgments, and some bankruptcies. However, we can only remove items that are legitimately disputable under law—we cannot remove accurate negative information that is being properly reported.

Do I need to cancel my credit cards during the repair process?

No, you should actually keep your existing credit cards open, as closing accounts can temporarily lower your score. We'll provide guidance on how to properly use your existing credit during the repair process to help build positive payment history while we work on removing negative items.
