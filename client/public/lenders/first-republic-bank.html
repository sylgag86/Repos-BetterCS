About First Republic Bank

First Republic Bank was founded in 1985 in San Francisco with a vision to create a different kind of bank—one focused on extraordinary client service, relationship-based banking, and a long-term perspective rather than short-term quarterly results. This approach was revolutionary at a time when the banking industry was trending toward standardization, automated systems, and reducing client interactions. The bank's founding team, led by Jim Herbert, established a distinctive model centered on hiring experienced bankers and empowering them to make decisions locally while developing deep relationships with clients in their communities. In the decades since its founding, First Republic has maintained this relationship-centered approach through multiple economic cycles and industry transformations, including navigating the 2008 financial crisis by maintaining strong credit quality when many competitors experienced significant loan losses. The bank has strategically expanded beyond its initial San Francisco base to urban coastal markets with similar demographics to its original clientele, establishing significant presences in New York, Boston, Los Angeles, and other metropolitan areas with concentrations of businesses and professionals seeking high-touch banking relationships. Throughout this growth, First Republic has maintained its founding principles of extraordinary service, focusing on client satisfaction rather than transaction volume, which has resulted in an unusually high client retention rate of over 95% annually and considerable organic growth through client referrals. Today, the bank operates as a full-service financial institution with expertise spanning commercial banking, private banking, wealth management, and residential lending, with each segment maintaining the personalized approach that has defined the bank since its inception.

What distinguishes First Republic Bank in the commercial banking sector is its unwavering commitment to relationship-based service backed by an organizational structure and culture specifically designed to deliver consistent, personalized attention to clients regardless of their size. Unlike national banks that typically segment clients by revenue tiers with frequently rotating relationship managers, First Republic assigns dedicated bankers who often maintain relationships with clients for many years or even decades, creating continuity that enables deeper understanding of client businesses and needs. This relationship model is complemented by the bank's unique cross-functional approach to client service, where business clients benefit from seamless integration between commercial banking, private banking, and wealth management services through a cohesive team structure rather than the siloed departments common at most institutions. First Republic bankers are empowered with significant autonomy in client management, credit decisions, and problem-solving, with local decision-making authority that allows for significantly faster responses to client needs compared to the centralized approval processes typical at larger institutions. The bank's service model is further distinguished by its focus on accessibility—clients receive direct contact information for their banking team, including cell phone numbers for senior bankers, representing a level of access rarely provided by competitors. This extraordinary service approach has resulted in industry-leading Net Promoter Scores consistently above 70, far exceeding banking industry averages that typically hover in the 30s. For businesses seeking a banking relationship characterized by personal attention, rapid responsiveness, and access to decision-makers rather than simply competitive rates or product features, First Republic offers a distinctive alternative to both national institutions and smaller community banks.

First Republic Bank offers a comprehensive range of commercial banking solutions designed for established businesses, professional service firms, and nonprofit organizations, with a particular focus on sectors including healthcare, legal services, technology, real estate, private equity, and education. Their Business Banking services are structured for companies with annual revenues from $2 million to $75 million, offering term loans from $250,000 to $5 million with customized terms typically ranging from 3-10 years, lines of credit from $250,000 to $5 million, and specialized equipment financing options. For larger Commercial & Institutional Banking clients with revenues exceeding $75 million, First Republic provides expanded solutions including term loans with customized structures up to $25 million, revolving lines of credit with flexible covenant arrangements, comprehensive treasury management systems with customized fraud protection controls, and specialized partnership capital call facilities for investment firms. The bank's Real Estate lending team offers specialized financing for commercial properties, including acquisition loans, construction financing, and term loans with typical loan-to-value ratios of 50-65% and terms extending 5-10 years. To qualify for business credit, companies typically need established operations with at least three years of positive financial performance, revenue of at least $2 million annually, and strong management teams, though First Republic evaluates borrowers holistically rather than applying rigid credit score thresholds. The approval process is notably relationship-driven—clients are assigned a dedicated banker who remains their primary contact throughout the relationship, supported by a team of specialists who contribute expertise while maintaining the relationship continuity. What particularly distinguishes First Republic's commercial lending approach is their commitment to maintaining this relationship consistency even during challenging economic periods or when businesses face temporary setbacks. Rather than transferring troubled loans to specialized workout departments as is common industry practice, First Republic keeps clients with their existing relationship team through financial challenges, allowing for more flexible and supportive solutions developed by bankers who understand the client's history and long-term potential. This approach has contributed to the bank's unusually strong credit performance through multiple economic cycles, with loan loss rates consistently well below industry averages.
