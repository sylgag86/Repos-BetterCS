About UBS Bank

UBS Bank traces its origins to 1862 when the Bank in Winterthur was founded in Switzerland, followed by the formation of the Swiss Banking Association in 1872, which later became Swiss Bank Corporation. The modern UBS emerged in 1998 through the historic merger of Swiss Bank Corporation and Union Bank of Switzerland, creating what was then the world's largest wealth management institution. This merger combined Union Bank's strong domestic Swiss banking franchise with Swiss Bank Corporation's international investment banking capabilities and extensive global wealth management network. Throughout its history, UBS has maintained significant operations across three core business divisions: Global Wealth Management, which serves high-net-worth and ultra-high-net-worth individuals worldwide; Investment Banking, which provides capital market services, advisory, and financing solutions; and Asset Management, which offers investment capabilities across asset classes for private and institutional clients. While UBS has experienced challenges, including significant losses during the 2008 financial crisis that required government intervention, the bank has undertaken substantial restructuring efforts since then, refocusing on its core wealth management capabilities while maintaining a more targeted investment banking operation. Today, UBS stands as one of the world's preeminent wealth management institutions, serving clients across the Americas, Europe, Asia, and emerging markets. The bank's strategic focus has increasingly shifted toward its wealth management business, which generates the majority of its profits and serves as the cornerstone of the organization. This evolution reflects both market dynamics and regulatory developments following the global financial crisis, with UBS embracing a more focused business model centered on providing comprehensive financial services to affluent clients worldwide.

What distinguishes UBS in the financial services landscape is its exceptional capabilities at the intersection of wealth management and sophisticated financing solutions—a specialized expertise developed through decades of serving ultra-high-net-worth individuals, family offices, and institutional clients globally. Unlike retail banks that primarily offer standardized consumer lending products or commercial banks focused on traditional business loans, UBS has developed a distinctive approach to financing that leverages clients' existing investment portfolios and complex asset structures to create tailored credit solutions. This unique positioning creates several distinct advantages: First, UBS can offer securities-backed lending that enables clients to access liquidity using their investment portfolios as collateral without disrupting long-term investment strategies or triggering tax consequences from liquidating positions. Second, their global presence provides cross-border financing capabilities that few competitors can match, allowing multinational families and businesses to access capital across different jurisdictions while navigating complex regulatory environments. Third, their integrated approach connects lending solutions with broader wealth planning, including estate planning, tax considerations, and investment strategy—creating a holistic approach to balance sheet management rather than treating financing as a standalone product. UBS's wealth management bankers operate more as financial advisors than traditional loan officers, possessing deep understanding of both client assets and sophisticated lending structures. While UBS typically serves clients with at least $5 million in investable assets (with many clients exceeding $25 million), their value proposition centers on providing bespoke financing solutions that optimize both sides of a client's balance sheet rather than simply offering the lowest possible interest rate. For affluent individuals and families seeking sophisticated financing strategies fully integrated with their broader wealth management approach, UBS represents a fundamentally different option than traditional commercial or retail lenders.

UBS Bank offers a comprehensive suite of lending and financing solutions tailored specifically for high-net-worth and ultra-high-net-worth individuals, family offices, and institutional clients. Their Securities-Backed Lending (SBL) serves as a flagship offering, allowing clients to borrow against diversified investment portfolios held at UBS with credit facilities ranging from $100,000 to $25+ million, at interest rates typically starting from SOFR plus 1.75-3.50% depending on portfolio composition and loan size, with advance rates usually between 50-80% of eligible assets depending on asset class, concentration, and volatility. For real estate financing, UBS provides both residential mortgage solutions with loan amounts from $500,000 to $50+ million for primary residences, vacation properties, and investment properties in major global markets, and commercial real estate financing for qualified clients with typical loan sizes from $3 million to $50+ million for income-producing properties. Their Tailored Lending solutions include structured credit facilities from $1 million to $100+ million customized around complex asset structures, including privately-held business interests, concentrated stock positions, and alternative investments, with each facility individually structured with customized terms, rates, and covenants based on the specific assets and client circumstances. For business owners and entrepreneurs, UBS offers private company financing options including acquisition financing, growth capital, and liquidity solutions with flexible structures that often incorporate both personal investment assets and business valuations in underwriting decisions. To qualify for UBS financing solutions, clients typically need minimum investable assets of $5 million with UBS (though many banking solutions are targeted toward clients with $25+ million), excellent credit profiles with credit scores generally above 700, and often existing relationships with the bank's wealth management division. The application process differs significantly from traditional commercial or retail lending, centered around a relationship-based approach where clients work directly with a UBS financial advisor and private banker to develop customized financing solutions as part of their comprehensive wealth management strategy, with approval decisions based on a holistic analysis of the client's overall financial situation, investment portfolio, and global banking needs rather than standardized underwriting formulas. What particularly distinguishes UBS's lending approach is their integration of financing solutions with broader wealth strategy, including tax planning, estate considerations, investment objectives, and cross-border needs, creating lending structures that optimize the entire balance sheet rather than viewing debt as an isolated transaction.
